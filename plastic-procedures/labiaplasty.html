LABIAL REDUCTION SURGERY ( LABIAPLASTY )

THE PROCEDURE

Some women develop large labia (vaginal lips) either when they go through puberty, or often after pregnancy. The procedure of labial reduction aims to reduce the size of the labia to give a more aesthetic appearance.

WHAT DOES IT INVOLVE?

The procedure is either performed under a local anaesthetic (with you awake) or under a general anaesthetic (with you asleep) usually as a day case (in and out of hospital on the same day) or occasionally with an overnight stay.

WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS?

Labial reduction surgery results in a "neater" looking appearance of your vagina. In addition, many women experience an improvement in their sex life following surgery.

HOW LONG DOES THE SURGERY TAKE?

The operation itself may take between 45-90 minutes, depending upon the extent of the surgery.

WHAT IS THE RECOVERY PERIOD?

Following surgery you will feel slightly sore and therefore you should take things gently for 1-2 weeks. You will need to perform daily showering of the wounds to keep the area as clean as possible. You will need to wear pads for 4-6 weeks after the operation.

HOW LONG BEFORE DAILY ACTIVITIES MAY BE RESUMED?

Most people are able to return to work at 2-weeks, although strenuous activity and sports should be avoided for around 4-6 weeks. Sexual intercourse should be avoided for up to 6-weeks following the operation to ensure maximal wound healing strength.

WHAT ARE THE SUCCESS RATES?

Labial reduction surgery has a high success rate.

WHAT ARE THE POSSIBLE COMPLICATIONS?

The most frequent problems that arise with labial reduction surgery relate to wound infection and delayed wound healing -this is due to the area of the body the surgery is taking place on, as well as the movement that occurs at the surgical site every time you walk (hence the need to take things gently immediately after the operation). Other important complications that may arise include clitoral sensitivity due to exposure; problematic tight scarring that may necessitate treatment; and bleeding -this is usually just onto the pad, but occasionally a haematoma can develop. This is a collection of blood within the labia which may necessitate a short return to the operating theatre to ensure it is treated properly.
