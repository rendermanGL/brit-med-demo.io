STITCHES FACELIFT

THE PROCEDURE

Different surgeons mean different things by the term "1-stitch" facelift. What the different techniques have in common is that they are skin tightening operations, that do not change the underlying soft tissue (the SMAS).

WHAT DOES IT INVOLVE?

The operation is done with you awake, under local anaesthetic. An incision is made behind the ear, according to pre-operative markings. The skin around the angle of the jaw is released, and deep stitches are placed to lift the underlying tissue in this area. The skin is then closed with fine stitches, which will be removed a week after surgery. An additional incision is sometimes made at the side burn area for further internal stitch placement.

WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS?

The 1-stitch facelift is a very good procedure in specific circumstances: it can be used to delay the timing of a full facelift (the results are very good, but are not guaranteed to last beyond 6-months to a year); it is good as a "maintenance" procedure 2-3 years after a facelift to re-tighten the skin; and it is good for a little tightening of the skin when you cannot afford the recovery time of a full facelift.

HOW LONG DOES THE SURGERY TAKE?

The surgery takes around an hour.

WHAT IS THE RECOVERY PERIOD?

You will have some swelling and bruising afterwards, which will fade after 1-2 weeks following surgery and the scars heal quickly. Bruising is usually confined to the sideburn and cheek area (and below the earlobe if an incision was used there), and can be covered with make-up.

HOW LONG BEFORE DAILY ACTIVITIES MAY BE RESUMED?

Rest is advised initially, and ideally you should sleep with 2 or 3 pillows initially so your head is elevated - this will help the swelling resolve. Most people are ready to go out and return to work the following day. Strenuous activity should be avoided for 3-4 weeks.

WHAT ARE THE SUCCESS RATES?

If done for the right reasons (as outlined above) and with realistic expectations, the one-stitch facelift is an excellent operation - it is safe, quick and has a short recovery time. However, it is not a substitute for a full facelift.

WHAT ARE THE POSSIBLE COMPLICATIONS?

Complication rates tend to be low for this procedure. They include localised bleeding and infection (as for any operation). Should there be a problem, appropriate treatment with dressings, antibiotics or a washout under local anaesthetic may be required.

The scar, which is designed to stay just within the hairline or around the earlobe, will be red for a few weeks before it starts to fade. Vitamin E scar cream (or similar) should be massaged into the scar on a daily basis from about a week after the stitches are removed to produce the best result.

There may be a degree of asymmetry between the two sides, however, it must be remembered that we all have different degrees of facial asymmetry to start with, due to variations in our bone structure and soft tissue distribution.
